Michelle King

Artist Statement

As an artist, I explore the boundaries between traditional and unconventional techniques, constantly seeking to reimagine the creative process. My practice spans painting, sculpture, and most recently, software development, though I do not approach any of these mediums in a conventional way. Without formal training in art, I embrace my position as an outsider, which allows me to break the rules with intention. I know art history well and have taught painting and drawing in my role as a primary teacher, but my fascination lies in reinterpreting established methods and pushing the limits of what constitutes art.

The uncanny and the potential of form captivate me. In my work, I focus on transforming materials, tools, and processes, often subverting expectations in the way I engage with them. For example, I explore how the process of making art can be augmented or even replaced by technology. This has led me to develop software, that interprets the relationship between color, form, and sound, allowing users to engage with art in ways that are simultaneously familiar and alien.

One of my latest projects merges color theory with digital tools. Through an app I created, users can upload images and translate color palettes into paint blends using a custom Java-based algorithm. This project is part of my larger exploration into the intersection of technology and art, where code becomes a new medium for creativity. I see this as a continuation of my broader inquiry into form, process, and the unknown spaces in between.

I am drawn to the notion of art as an evolving practice—one that is not confined by traditional methods, but instead constantly expanding to include new forms of engagement, experimentation, and interaction.
